🔍 Ask Noto anything

Noto answers from the company's real knowledge — the wiki, docs, chats, candidate files, firm intel and training material — and cites what it used.

What's our policy on X? Where is the doc that covers Y?
Summarize what we know about [client/topic].
Who said what about [project] in the team chats?

Works in your DM or by @mentioning Noto in a group. Deeper questions take a minute or two — you'll see progress in the answer card. A 👍 on a good answer teaches Noto that the route it took was right.

📩 Your email

Ask about your own inbox

/mail did they ever reply about the contract?
check my email — what did I tell her about comp?

1:1 DM only. Noto searches your mailbox (full threads, years back) and cites dates + senders. Nobody can query anyone else's mailbox — not even admins.

Auto-drafted replies — the review card

When an email arrives with you in To: (never CC, never newsletters) and it needs an answer, Noto drafts a reply and DMs you a card: what they wrote, the draft, and a confidence score — 🟢 85+ routine, safe to send 🟡 60–84 worth a skim 🔴 <60 review carefully — plus a “missing” line for anything it couldn't know. For substantive emails Noto researches first: your past exchanges and the company knowledge base — the note shows what it consulted.

  • Send — goes out exactly as shown: reply-all, inside the same thread, your signature.
  • Discard — deletes the draft.
  • Edit — opens Lark Mail; the draft is in your Drafts.
  • Or just reply to the card: say “send” / “discard”, or describe a change — “shorter, and confirm Thursday” — and Noto redoes the draft and sends a fresh card.

Drafts follow the house playbook — how VP actually answers, mined nightly from the team's sent mail — then your own voice on top. Facts Noto can't verify are never invented; they go to the card note. Nothing ever sends without your tap.

The playbook (admins)

Admin panel → Playbook tab: every learned response pattern with the exact exchange it came from, and Keep / Retire controls. Quick look in chat: /playbook.

💬 Talking to Noto

  • Reply = context. Use Lark's Reply on any Noto message and your reply carries that message as context — follow up on old answers days later, naturally.
  • Teach it: start a message with feedback: or remember this: and it's filed for the learning loop (admins review what becomes a standing rule).
  • Praise works: 👍 on an answer saves the successful route for similar questions.
  • Redo drafts: reply to a draft card with the change you want, or redo q#12: friendlier from anywhere in the DM.

⚙️ What runs automatically

  • Nightly comb (3:30am): re-syncs wikis, docs and chats; refreshes indexes; mines chat Q&A nuggets.
  • Morning reminders: your task list, DM'd when it's 8am in your timezone.
  • Auto-drafts: new To-addressed email checked every 5 minutes (mail users).
  • Playbook mining (1:30am): distills more of the team's sent mail into the response playbook.
  • Expenses: DM a receipt photo/file → logged to the reimbursement Base. Screenshots of event invites → calendar entries (after your confirm).

🔒 Privacy & limitations — read this once

  • Mailbox isolation is absolute. Email answers and drafts only ever come from your own mailbox, only in your 1:1 DM. No cross-mailbox queries, no exceptions.
  • Noto never deletes. No document, file, message, record or wiki page — enforced in code at startup. The single exception: its own mail drafts when you hit Discard.
  • Nothing sends itself. Every email draft waits for your explicit Send.
  • Confidence is a triage hint, not a guarantee — read 🟡 and 🔴 drafts before sending. Sensitive topics (pricing, legal terms, rejections) are always capped orange on purpose.
  • Knowledge freshness: docs/chats sync nightly (email every few minutes) — today's brand-new doc may not be indexed until tomorrow morning.
  • Group-chat history from before Noto joined a group is invisible to it.
  • Company data only. Noto answers from indexed company knowledge; it doesn't browse your personal accounts or invent facts — “I don't know” is a feature.
  • Email features require setup per user — mailbox in the config + a one-click authorization.
